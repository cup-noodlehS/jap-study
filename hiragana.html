Part 2

Dakuten & Handakuten

Two little marks change consonants. Master these and you've added 25 sounds without learning new shapes.

How they work

  • Dakuten ゛ (two small dashes, top-right) voices the consonant. か ka → が ga.
  • Handakuten ゜ (a small circle, top-right) only goes on the h-row to make the p-row. は ha → ぱ pa.
  • The shape doesn't change. Just the mark. That means once you know the base chart, the dakuten chart is almost free.

Combinations & Long Sounds

Three rules that let you read almost any beginner word: yōon, small つ, and long vowels.

Rule 1 · Yōon

Small や, ゆ, よ make blended sounds

When a kana from the -i column (き, し, ち, etc.) is followed by a small ゃ ゅ ょ, they combine into one syllable.

Rule 2 · The small つ doubles the next consonant

  • A small っ (different from regular つ) means hold a silent beat, then double the next consonant.
  • がっこう → gakkou (school) — note the double "k"
  • きって → kitte (stamp) — double "t"
  • いっぽん → ippon (one [long object]) — double "p"

Rule 3 · Long vowels

  • Vowel sounds can be doubled by adding another vowel after the kana.
  • aa → ああ ・ ii → いい ・ uu → うう
  • ee is usually written えい: せんせい = sensei (teacher)
  • oo is usually written おう: こうこう = koukou (high school). Exceptions: おおきい ookii, とおい tooi.
  • The length matters! おばさん (obasan, aunt) vs おばあさん (obaasan, grandmother).
Part 4

How to Learn It Fast

A 7-day plan if you're truly starting from zero

  • Day 1: a-row + ka-row (10 kana). Drill until you can read them in random order in under 5 seconds each.
  • Day 2: sa-row + ta-row (10 more). Review day 1.
  • Day 3: na-row + ha-row. Review days 1–2.
  • Day 4: ma-row + ya-row + ra-row. Review everything.
  • Day 5: wa-row + ん + dakuten/handakuten. You now know every basic kana.
  • Day 6: yōon, small っ, long vowels. Start reading short words.
  • Day 7: Practice with the flashcards in the section below.

Mistakes everyone makes

  • Confusing わ wa and れ re and ね ne and ぬ nu. They all share a left-side spine. Look at the right side: わ is plain, れ has a hook out, ね is a loop, ぬ is a closed knot.
  • Confusing き ki and さ sa. Mirror images of each other. ki has TWO horizontal lines, sa has ONE.
  • Confusing は ha, ほ ho, け ke. Look at the bottom-right: ha is open, ho has an extra mark, ke has no extra at all.
  • Forgetting that は is pronounced "wa" when used as the topic particle. Same for へ → "e" as the direction particle, を → "o" as the object particle.
  • Skipping the long vowels. "おばさん" and "おばあさん" mean totally different things. Always count vowels.
